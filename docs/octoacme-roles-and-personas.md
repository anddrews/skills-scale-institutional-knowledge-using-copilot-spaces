# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Portfolio Manager

### Role Summary
Portfolio Managers oversee alignment of project objectives with organizational strategy and manage resource allocation across multiple projects. They ensure that the project portfolio delivers maximum business value.

### Responsibilities
- Align project initiatives with organizational strategic goals
- Manage resource allocation and capacity planning across projects
- Prioritize projects within the portfolio based on value and strategic fit
- Monitor portfolio health and performance metrics
- Resolve resource conflicts and dependencies between projects
- Report portfolio status to executive leadership

### Goals
- Maximize return on investment across the project portfolio
- Ensure strategic alignment of all initiatives
- Optimize resource utilization across the organization
- Balance short-term delivery with long-term strategic objectives

### Typical Communication
- Monthly portfolio reviews with executive leadership
- Quarterly strategic alignment sessions
- Regular coordination with Project Managers and Executive Sponsors
- Resource allocation meetings with functional leads

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers coordinate change control processes, ensure effective communication of changes, and manage change-related risks. They facilitate smooth transitions and minimize disruption to operations.

### Responsibilities
- Establish and oversee change control processes
- Assess impact of proposed changes on people, processes, and technology
- Develop change communication and training plans
- Coordinate with stakeholders to manage change adoption
- Track change-related risks and mitigation strategies
- Facilitate change advisory board meetings when needed

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from changes to operations and users
- Ensure high adoption rates for new processes and tools
- Maintain compliance with change management policies
- Reduce change-related incidents and rollbacks

### Typical Communication
- Change advisory board meetings
- Impact assessments and change requests
- Training materials and adoption guides
- Regular updates to Project Manager and affected stakeholders

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define quality standards and test protocols, verify that deliverables meet requirements, and coordinate quality activities with engineering teams. They are accountable for product quality.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards, test strategies, and acceptance criteria
- Design and execute test plans (functional, integration, performance)
- Coordinate testing activities with development and product teams
- Track and report on quality metrics and defect trends
- Ensure deliverables meet defined quality gates before release
- Advocate for quality best practices and continuous improvement

### Goals
- Deliver products that meet or exceed quality standards
- Minimize production defects and customer-reported issues
- Achieve high test coverage and automation rates
- Foster a quality-first culture within the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Project Manager and Product Owner
- Daily coordination with development teams on defect resolution
- Quality status reports in sprint reviews and release meetings
- Post-release quality assessments and improvement recommendations

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons serve as the primary point of contact for stakeholders, aggregate and prioritize feedback, and promote transparency throughout the project lifecycle. They bridge the gap between the project team and broader stakeholder community.

### Responsibilities
- Serve as main interface between project team and stakeholders
- Collect, consolidate, and prioritize stakeholder feedback
- Facilitate stakeholder meetings and ensure representation
- Communicate project status, decisions, and changes to stakeholders
- Manage stakeholder expectations and address concerns
- Maintain stakeholder contact lists and engagement plans

### Goals
- Ensure stakeholder voices are heard and considered
- Maintain high stakeholder satisfaction and trust
- Prevent surprises and manage expectations proactively
- Build strong relationships across the stakeholder community

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder briefings and updates
- Feedback sessions and surveys
- Coordination with Project Manager and Communications Lead
- Escalation of critical stakeholder concerns to project leadership
